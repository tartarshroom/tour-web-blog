About the Tour

Our Educational Tour 2026, held from April 5–12, 2026, provided us with valuable real-world experiences through company visits, historical explorations, and cultural activities. The tour allowed us to witness how different industries operate while also appreciating the rich history and beauty of the Philippines.

Throughout the seven-day journey, we visited various companies, tourist destinations, and historical landmarks across Manila, Tagaytay, and Baguio. Beyond academic learning, the tour strengthened our teamwork, communication, and appreciation for new experiences outside the classroom.

Daily Experiences

Day 1 – Manila Arrival & Tour

Our first day began with our arrival in Manila followed by an exciting city tour. We explored historical destinations such as Intramuros, Fort Santiago, Rizal Park, and Manila Cathedral, where we learned about the country’s rich history and heritage. We also visited Mall of Asia to relax, enjoy the surroundings, and experience the vibrant atmosphere of the city.

Day 2 – Quezon & Makati

On the second day, we visited Hytec Power Inc. in Novaliches during the morning and OpenText Philippines Inc. in Makati in the afternoon. These company visits introduced us to professional work environments, technology-based operations, and the importance of innovation and teamwork in the industry.

Day 3 – Pasay & Taguig

Day three focused on observing real-world operations in both government and private sectors. In the morning, we visited the MMDA Command Center to learn about traffic monitoring and management systems. In the afternoon, we proceeded to Teleperformance Philippines Inc. in Taguig, where we gained insights into the BPO industry and customer service operations.

Day 4 – Tech Visits

On the fourth day, we visited Top Peg Animation and Creative Studio, Inc. in Las Piñas and Microsourcing Philippines Inc. in Libis, Quezon City. These visits allowed us to learn about animation production, creative design processes, and the modern corporate work environment in the outsourcing industry.

Day 5 – Tagaytay

Day five was dedicated to sightseeing and relaxation in Tagaytay. We visited People’s Park and Tagaytay Sky Ranch, where we enjoyed the cool climate, scenic landscapes, and memorable bonding moments with classmates and instructors.

Day 6–7 – Baguio

During the final days of our educational tour, we explored the beautiful city of Baguio. We visited famous attractions including Strawberry Farm, Bell Church, the Philippine Military Academy, The Mansion, Mines View Park, and Burnham Park. The experience allowed us to appreciate the culture, environment, and unique atmosphere of the Summer Capital of the Philippines.
